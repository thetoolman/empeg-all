Speaker Connections

Now that we have your music collection in order, we can get to work on your speaker connections.  The first step is to verify that you have the correct wires going to the correct speakers.  This test is pretty simple and may even seem a bit silly, but it only takes a few seconds to perform and may save some headaches down the road.
 
Checking All Four Channels
1.  Insert your player into your car's sled and apply power.  Start playing a random song.  Any song will do so you might as well pick one that you like!  Set the volume to your normal listening level.

2. Using the rotary encoder (aka volume knob) on your empeg mk2/RioCar (or access the Sound menu with your empeg mk1), bring up the Fader Control and set the fader all the way to the front.  You should only hear sound from your front speakers.   If you don't hear your music from only the front speakers, turn off the player and set the car's ignition to the "Off" position.  Now you can safely check the speaker connections on your amp to fix the problem.  If everything is fine, continue to Step 3.

3.  With the Fader Control still set all the way to Front, use the rotary controller to bring up the Balance Control and set the balance all the way to the Left.  The music should only be playing from the front-left speaker.   Next, set the balance all the way to the Right.  The music should only be playing from the front-right speaker.   If either of these tests don't check out, turn off the player and set the car's ignition to the "off" position.  Now you can safely check the speaker connections on your amp to fix the problem.  If everything is fine, continue to Step 4.

4.  Repeat steps 2 and 3, except this time set the Fader Control all the way to the Rear.  You'll be performing the same tests to ensure you're hearing the music from the proper speakers.  Just be sure to power off the player and turn the ignition to "Off" position before you make any changes or you might be in for a nasty shock!

5.  When you're done, be sure to set the Fader and Balance controls back to their default center positions.
 

Hopefully the local Best Buy didn't create too much work for you there!  But now that we've got the speaker connections straightened out, we can move onto getting the best signal-to-noise ratio from your speakers.
