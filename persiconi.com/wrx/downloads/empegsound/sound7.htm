Left/Right Time Alignment

In the previous section, we used the Balance Control to compensated for the difference in volume between the left and right speakers while sitting in your car.  However, in addition to relative volume, our brains judge the location of a sound based on the difference in time that is takes a sound to reach one ear compared to another. 

Christian Hack created what is possibly the most underrated tool available to empeg owners to fix this problem: Left/Right Time Alignment.  His program intercepts the samples of the audio signal and applies a tiny amount of delay to either the right or left channel.  It delays the speaker closest to you, so that the sound from each speaker arrives to your ears at the same time.  Making things even better, starting with version 200, all Hijack kernels have this feature built in.  Installation and setup is a snap.

Note: As with the Balance Control, adjustments made to the Time Alignment benefit only the person in the driver's seat and will in fact result in worse sound for passengers.
 
If you don't already have Mark Lord's Hijack installed on your player, you will need to do so for this part of the tutorial.  It is a very easy and safe procedure, instructions here.

Time Alignment Setup:
1.  Download imagingtest.mp3 (Right-click, Save As..) and add it to your "empeg sound - Misc. Files" playlist.  Select this playlist and make sure it is marked "Ignore as Child" so that it won't get played during normal player use.

2. Synchronize with your player and go find your car.

3.  (Recommended: Drive your car to a quiet location.)  Insert your empeg into your car's sled and apply power. 

4.  Using the rotary controller, access the player's Sound menu and apply the following settings:

  • Make sure Balance is set to the same value you set earlier.
  • Set Fader to the middle.
  • Set Loudness to 0.0dB.
  • Beep Level to 0%.
  • Pitchshift to 100% (3.00alpha software only).
  • Auto Volume Adjust to "Off" (3.00alpha software only).
  • Enter the Equalizer menu and select a preset #1 "Flat".

5.  Enter the Hijack menu by pressing and holding the rotary controller to apply the following settings:

  • Set Tone: Bass Adjust and Tone: Treble Adjust to "Off".
  • Set Auto Volume Adjust to "Off".

6.  Browse your playlists and find a song to use for this imaging test.  You'll want to find a song that has prominent vocals for the best results.  Begin playback.

7.  Enter the Hijack menu by pressing and holding the rotary controller and select Left/Right Time Alignment.  You'll notice an interface that lets you adjust the time delay in msec (or represented in the distance the listener is away from the dead center of the vehicle.)

For now, we're going to ignore the numbers on the screen.  The best way to calibrate this setting is to close your eyes and turn the rotary knob to the left or right concentrating on the following points:

  • Make your adjustments slowly, one click at a time.
  • Keep your eyes closed and focus on the music.
  • The ideal setting will leave the music sounding like it is right in front of you.
  • There is no way to measure for the perfect amount of delay, so make it sound good to you.
  • When done, hit the round "Up" button to save your settings.

It may take a few tries to get this right and you might find that your first choice in music wasn't ideal.  If you're seated on the left, your adjustments will be made to the Left (and vice versa) but because all cars are different, this may not be the case.

8.  To test your Balance and Left/Right Time Alignment settings, begin playback of the imagingtest.mp3 track you downloaded in Step 1.  The seven snare drum hits are placed evenly across the sound stage with the fourth hit being in the center.  The sequence is repeated twice.  If you are not happy with the results, repeat Step 7.  Note: This track is NOT recommended for calibrating the Time Alignment.
 

Now that we've adjusted for the placement of your speakers, we can now begin work on their frequency response.
