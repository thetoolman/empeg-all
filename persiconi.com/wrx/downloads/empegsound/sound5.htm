Phase Test

In this next set of tests, we'll be verifying that all of your speakers are "in-phase" with one another.  Essentially, when two speakers are out of phase with one another, one speaker is pushing while the other speaker is pulling.  This problem is actually quite common in some home and car audio setups because it is caused by the simple mistake of having one speaker's negative and positive connections switched.. 

For a graphic representation, the following diagram shows two sounds waves that are 180 degrees out of phase from another:

Because these sound waves represent pure tones, playing both waves at the same time would cause them to completely cancel each other.   When playing music, having an out of phase speaker can cause a distorted stereo image as well as cancellation at some frequencies.  Luckily, checking for this condition and fixing it are very easy to do.  To learn more about Phase Relationships, click here.
 
Phase Test Procedure
There are plenty of guides on the internet that suggest hooking up a small battery to your speaker wires to perform this test.  That procedure can damage your speakers and requires two people to do it safely.  Instead, we're going to use a simple test track.

1. Download speakerpolaritycheck.mp3 (Right-click, Save As..) and load the file onto your empeg in a new playlist called "empeg sound - Misc. Files."  Select this playlist and mark it "Ignore as Child" so that it won't get played during normal player use.

2. Synchronize with your player and go find your car.

3.  (Recommended: Drive your car to a quiet location.)  Insert your empeg into your car's sled and apply power. 

4.  Using the rotary encoder (volume knob), adjust the Fader Control all the way to the Front.

5.  Using the rotary controller, access the player's Sound menu and apply the following settings:

  • Set Balance to its middle settings.
  • Set Loudness to 0.0dB.
  • Beep Level to 0%.
  • Pitchshift to 100% (3.00alpha software only).
  • Auto Volume Adjust to "Off" (3.00alpha software only).
  • Enter the Equalizer menu and select a preset #1 "Flat".

6.  Enter the Hijack menu by pressing and holding the rotary controller to apply the following settings: (If you do not have HiJack installed, you can skip this step).

  • Set Left/Right Time Alignment to "Off".
  • Set Tone: Bass Adjust and Tone: Treble Adjust to "Off".
  • Set Auto Volume Adjust to "Off".

7.  Select your "Test Tones" playlist that includes the track you downloaded in Step #1 and begin playback.   The track will walk you through the test procedure. 

8.  If you need to make any adjustments to your speaker wires, turn off the player and set the car's ignition to the "off" position.  Now you can safely check the speaker connections on your amp to fix the problem.  Repeat Step #6 to verify you've fixed the problem.

9.  Once the front speakers are in phase, adjust the Fader Control all the way to the Rear and repeat Steps 6 & 7 for the rear speakers.

Optional:  Sometimes, when speakers are not mounted close to each other (i.e., component speakers that have the midrange drivers on the doors and tweeters up in the dash), reversing the polarity on tweeters or mids makes the system sound better because it makes up for phase differences due to distance.   Try different combinations and see what sounds better.
 

Again, this was another test that took longer to write that to perform!  At this point, we can now move onto the next step..
