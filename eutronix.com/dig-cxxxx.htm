Update: The DIG-CXXX has now been tested and verified to work with Kenwood Music Keg MP3 audio systems. A simple 5 minute procedure is all that is needed to enable the DIG-CXXXX to provide pristine S/PDIF digital audio through Coaxial or TOSlink interfaces.

By using digital signals, your music will be less susceptible to interference picked up in ordinary analog cables. What's more, by using a digital interface, your music can be converted from the digital format that all digital audio files such as MP3s are in to analog by much higher quality external circuitry than is present on the MP3 player's own circuitry. Depending on the converter used, your analog output levels can now more closely match the levels of other audio equipment in your system. Use a quality digital to analog converter and you won't be disappointed.

No expense has been spared in the design. Gold plated connectors abound. Unlike most computer sound cards, the DIG-CXXXX is equipped with an isolation transformer to eliminate the possibility of distracting ground loops from appearing in your music.

The sound card is available in one of three basic configurations:

Coaxial TOSlink I2S
The default standard for digital transmission of audio over cable. Timing and audio data are combined and conveyed over 75 ohm coaxial cable using electrical pulses in the S/PDIF format. For best reliability and sound quality, only high quality video cables or cables labeled for digital audio use should be used. This format is found on many CD players, digital tape recorders, Mini disc recorders, and some mobile audio equipment. It conveys the same S/PDIF digital audio standard used in the coaxial format, but using pulses of light transmitted over special TOSlink digital fiber optic cabling. It is especially well-suited for applications subject to strong electrical interference to which it is completely immune. This standard is considered the best method of the three for transmitting digital audio because it maintains separation of the various timing signals from the digital audio data until the signal reaches the digital to analog converter. By maintaining this separation, jitter, a timing error that sometimes degrades digital audio transmissions is reduced. Use this format if you have the ancillary equipment and are concerned about getting the ultimate in sound quality.
